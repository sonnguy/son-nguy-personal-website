## Key Product Decisions

### Business-first Positioning

The site prioritizes business domains such as Payments, Martech, Analytics, Product Engineering, and Customer Experience over a generic technology stack. Technologies support the story but are not the primary message.

### Not a Traditional Portfolio

The site intentionally avoids project galleries, GitHub repository showcases, and excessive technology-wall presentation. Featured work is presented as evidence of business-domain experience and product ownership.

### Content-first Design

The visual direction should remain clean, professional, modern, and executive-facing, inspired by Stripe, Linear, and Vercel. Design choices should improve clarity and credibility without competing with the content.

### Resume as Source of Truth

All experience, projects, companies, metrics, and claims should be grounded in the approved resume content and its structured repository data. Website copy may reframe that information for clarity but must not extend it with unverified claims.

### Recruiter Conversion Priority

The site should help recruiters and hiring managers quickly understand Son’s market value and decide whether to start an interview process. Content hierarchy and calls to action should support fast scanning, resume access, and direct contact.
